## === WEBTOON VISUAL STYLE GUIDE ===

### CORE AESTHETIC
Modern Korean webtoon style with semi-realistic character proportions, clean digital rendering, and cinematic composition. Balances detailed character work with simplified but atmospheric backgrounds. Strong focus on emotional storytelling through visual language.

### SHOT COMPOSITION

**Establishing shots:**
- Wide environmental panels with moderate background detail
- Vertical format emphasizing height and depth
- Clear spatial relationships between characters and environment
- Atmospheric perspective to create depth

**Medium shots:**
- Character framing from waist/chest up for dialogue scenes
- Comfortable negative space around characters
- Background often simplified or blurred to focus on characters
- Natural, conversational positioning

**Close-ups:**
- Face and upper shoulders for emotional beats
- High detail on facial features, especially eyes
- Soft focus or minimal background
- Used for reaction shots and intimate moments

**Action shots:**
- Dynamic angles and perspective
- Motion blur and speed lines for movement
- Impact frames with bold effects
- Varied panel heights to create rhythm

### VISUAL BEATS RHYTHM

**Detail beats:**
- Close-ups of hands, objects, facial expressions
- Soft lighting and shallow depth of field
- Textural details on clothing, accessories
- Creates intimate, contemplative moments

**Action beats:**
- Bold, dynamic compositions
- Strong directional lines
- High contrast and saturated colors
- Quick visual rhythm with varied panel sizes

**Reaction beats:**
- Character close-ups with expressive faces
- Simplified backgrounds or gradient fills
- Emotional color overlays (warm for positive, cool for tension)
- Strategic use of white space for impact

### LINE ART

**Line weight**: Variable weight with confident, clean strokes
- Thicker outlines for character contours
- Thinner lines for interior details and textures
- Consistent line quality across panels

**Line quality**: Digital, smooth, and polished
- Clean vector-like quality
- Minimal sketchy or rough lines
- Precise and controlled

**Detail density**: 
- High detail on character faces and hands
- Moderate detail on clothing and hair
- Simplified but structured backgrounds
- Strategic detail placement for visual hierarchy

**Stylistic approach**:
- Characters: Bold outlines with refined interior details
- Backgrounds: Lighter, more atmospheric linework
- Effects: Varied line styles for motion and emotion

### COLOR PALETTE

**Dominant colors**: 
- Warm neutrals (beiges, soft browns, creams) for everyday scenes
- Saturated accent colors for emotional emphasis
- Cool blues and purples for night/tension scenes
- Soft pastels for romantic/tender moments

**Color temperature**: 
- Warm bias for intimate, comfortable scenes
- Cool shift for dramatic or tense moments
- Golden hour lighting frequently used
- Temperature shifts signal mood changes

**Saturation strategy**:
- Moderate saturation for realistic feel
- Desaturated backgrounds to push characters forward
- Highly saturated for emotional peaks
- Gradient saturation for atmospheric depth

**Color coding**:
- Warm glow = intimacy, comfort, romance
- Cool tones = tension, distance, melancholy
- High saturation = emotional intensity
- Desaturation = flashbacks, memories, detachment

**Contrast levels**:
- Moderate to high contrast for readability
- Characters pop against softer backgrounds
- Strategic low contrast for atmospheric effects

### LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE

**Light direction**:
- Natural top-down and side lighting
- Window light frequently featured
- Soft ambient fill light
- Dramatic backlighting for silhouettes

**Mood lighting**:
- Golden hour warmth for positive emotions
- Cool blue for night and introspection
- Harsh shadows for tension
- Soft, diffused light for tenderness

**Shadow rendering**:
- Soft gradient shadows with subtle edges
- Colored shadows (not pure black)
- Shadow shapes suggest light source
- Minimal hard-edge shadows

**Highlight style**:
- Soft specular highlights on eyes (signature sparkle)
- Rim lighting on hair
- Subtle glow effects
- Screen-like highlights on reflective surfaces

**Atmospheric effects**:
- Soft focus blur for depth
- Light particles (dust, sparkles) for mood
- Gradient overlays for time of day
- Subtle vignetting for focus

**Time of day indicators**:
- Warm orange/yellow = morning/afternoon
- Cool blue/purple = evening/night
- Neutral balanced = midday
- Color temperature is primary time indicator

### DEPTH & SPATIAL DESIGN

**Layering**:
- Clear foreground/middleground/background separation
- Characters in sharp focus
- Progressive blur for background layers
- Atmospheric perspective with color desaturation

**Depth cues**:
- Size scaling for distance
- Overlap for spatial relationships
- Atmospheric haze for far elements
- Perspective lines for architectural spaces

**Focus control**:
- Sharp focus on narrative subjects
- Gaussian blur for background depth
- Selective focus for emotional emphasis
- Depth of field simulation

**Spatial compression**:
- Semi-realistic depth
- Not overly flat or overly deep
- Comfortable spatial reading
- Cinematic depth of field

### CHARACTER RENDERING STYLE

**Proportions**:
- Semi-realistic with slight idealization
- 7-8 head heights for adults
- Slender, elegant builds
- Expressive hands and gestures

**Facial features**:
- Large, detailed eyes with multiple highlights
- Simplified nose (often just shadow indication)
- Defined lips with subtle shading
- Expressive eyebrows
- Detailed hair with flow and volume

**Expression range**:
- Subtle to moderately exaggerated
- Realistic emotional nuance
- Occasional chibi/SD for comedy relief
- Eyes carry most emotional weight

**Anatomy approach**:
- Realistic skeletal structure
- Slightly idealized proportions
- Natural poses and gestures
- Attention to hand anatomy

**Clothing/texture detail**:
- Moderate fabric detail
- Realistic folds and draping
- Patterns rendered with care
- Texture suggested through shading

### BACKGROUND TREATMENT

**Detail philosophy**:
- Stylized realism
- Enough detail to establish setting
- Simplified compared to characters
- Atmospheric rather than photorealistic

**Texture vs flat**:
- Mix of textured and flat areas
- Gradient fills for sky and walls
- Subtle textures for surfaces
- Clean, organized visual hierarchy

**Perspective accuracy**:
- Correct but not overly technical
- Comfortable, readable perspective
- Expressive when needed for drama
- Architectural elements well-structured

**Environment storytelling**:
- Backgrounds support narrative
- Props and details add context
- Lighting sets mood
- Minimal but meaningful detail

### VISUAL EFFECTS & TECHNIQUES

**Motion indicators**:
- Speed lines for fast movement
- Motion blur for dynamic action
- Multiple position frames for impact
- Directional lines for emphasis

**Emotional effects**:
- Sparkles and light particles for positive emotions
- Dark auras or shadows for negative emotions
- Flower petals for romance
- Abstract shapes for shock/surprise

**Screentone/patterns**:
- Digital gradient screens
- Subtle dot patterns for texture
- Gradient overlays for atmosphere
- Minimal traditional screentone aesthetic

**Panel transitions**:
- Seamless vertical scroll
- Fade transitions between scenes
- White space for pacing
- Occasional full-bleed panels for impact

**Text integration**:
- Clean, modern speech bubbles
- White bubbles with subtle shadows
- SFX integrated into art
- Readable, well-spaced typography

### EMOTIONAL VISUAL LANGUAGE

**Intimacy markers**:
- Soft focus and warm lighting
- Close framing
- Gentle color palette
- Minimal background detail

**Tension indicators**:
- Cool color temperature
- Harsh shadows or high contrast
- Tight cropping
- Desaturated palette

**Joy/lightness**:
- Bright, saturated colors
- Soft edges and glows
- Open, airy compositions
- Warm lighting

**Drama/intensity**:
- High contrast lighting
- Dynamic angles
- Bold, saturated colors
- Sharp focus and detail

**Visual style shifts**:
- Color temperature changes with emotion
- Saturation increases for emotional peaks
- Background simplification for focus
- Lighting direction supports mood

### ARTISTIC IDENTITY

**Overall aesthetic**: Korean webtoon (manhwa) digital style
**Digital rendering**: Clean, polished digital painting with soft brushwork
**Stylistic consistency**: Highly consistent across panels with intentional variations for effect
**Distinctive signatures**: 
- Signature eye sparkles
- Soft gradient backgrounds
- Warm color bias
- Cinematic composition
- Clean, modern aesthetic

## Usage Guidelines

### For Scene Generation

Combine this style guide with specific scene descriptions:

**Formula**: `[Base Style] + [Specific Scene Details]`

**Example**:
```
Korean webtoon style, medium shot, reaction beat, clean confident linework, warm palette with soft shadows, window light from left, moderate depth with blurred background, semi-realistic proportions with expressive eyes, simplified cafe interior + young woman with long black hair in casual sweater, worried expression while looking at phone, afternoon setting
```

### Style Consistency Tips

1. **Always include**: "Korean webtoon style" as base
2. **Specify shot type**: Establishes framing and composition
3. **Define visual beat**: Guides detail level and pacing
4. **Set color mood**: Temperature and saturation for emotion
5. **Describe lighting**: Direction and quality for atmosphere
6. **Character details**: Specific features while maintaining style
7. **Background level**: Match detail to narrative focus
8. **Emotional tone**: Final touch for cohesive mood

### Adaptation for Different Scenes

**Romantic scenes**: Warm colors, soft lighting, close shots, sparkle effects
**Tense scenes**: Cool colors, harsh shadows, tight crops, high contrast
**Action scenes**: Dynamic angles, motion effects, saturated colors
**Quiet moments**: Soft focus, warm neutrals, detail beats, intimate framing
